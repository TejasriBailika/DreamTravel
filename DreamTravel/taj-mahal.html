The Taj Mahal - A Symbol of Love

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this breathtaking marble mausoleum attracts millions of visitors from all over the world.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit the Taj Mahal is between October and March, when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Top Attractions Nearby:

  • Agra Fort: A majestic fort offering a stunning view of the Taj Mahal.
  • Mehtab Bagh: A perfect spot for sunset photography of the Taj Mahal.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: A UNESCO World Heritage site and a former Mughal capital.

Popular Activities:

  • Guided tours of the Taj Mahal, exploring its architectural marvels.
  • Photography sessions at sunrise or sunset for the best view.
  • Shopping for local handicrafts and souvenirs, especially marble replicas of the Taj Mahal.

Visitor Tips:

  • Arrive early to avoid the large crowds, especially during peak seasons.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking, as the area is large and requires a lot of walking.
  • Respect the monument; no food or loud noises are allowed inside.
